1. The House That Jack Built

Watch for free

The main theme of The House That Jack Built is the relationship between art, evil, and morality. The film treats Jack’s murders as a twisted form of artistic expression — he sees killing as “creating art,” which forces the audience to question whether art can justify cruelty or if beauty can exist in something immoral.

It also explores the nature of evil and guilt. Jack represents a person who intellectualizes his crimes, using logic and aesthetics to hide from his conscience. Throughout the film, he debates with a mysterious figure (Verge) about whether his actions have meaning, symbolizing humanity’s struggle between reason and morality.

2.Takopi's Original Sin

The main theme of Takopi’s Original Sin is the contrast between innocence and human suffering

— how good intentions, when paired with ignorance, can lead to tragedy. On the surface, Takopi is a pure, happy alien who only wants to spread joy. But when he meets Shizuka, a girl burdened by abuse, bullying, and loneliness, his inability to understand real pain causes him to make things worse, not better. This reflects one of the story’s deepest ideas: true happiness cannot exist without understanding sadness.
